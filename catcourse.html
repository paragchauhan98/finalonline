About CAT

“The Common Admission Test (CAT)[2] is a computer based test for admission in a graduate management program. The test consists of three sections Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension (VARC), Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning (DILR) and Quantitative Ability (QA). Overall it’s a three hour online exam divided into one hour each section. The Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) started this exam and use the test for selecting students for their business administration programs (MBA or PGDM). The test is conducted every year by one of the IIMs based on a policy of rotation. In August 2011, it is announced that Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and Indian Institute of Science (IISc) would also use the CAT scores, instead of the Joint Management Entrance Test (JMET), to select students for their management programmes starting with the 2012-15 batch.[3] ” “Before 2009, CAT was a paper based test conducted on a single day for all candidates.CAT or Common Admission Test is one of the prestigious and significant entrance tests for students planning to build their career in management and business. Common Admission Test is held once a year by the Indian Institute of Management. Candidates who hold a 3 years bachelors degree in any stream can appear for this exam. However, he or she must secure at least 50% marks in their bachelor’s exam. This entrance test examines four major aspects of the candidates. These are: Quantitative Aptitude Verbal ability Data interpretation Logical reasoning Candidates must pass each of these groups in order to qualify in the exam. Qualifying in the CAT entrance exam will ensure the candidate a seat in a management curse. However, the best ones will be able to secure their seats in the most prestigious institutes of the country. There is no age limit for a candidate to appear for the CAT exam.
